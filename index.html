What Are Strategy Preferred Equities?

Bitcoin-native preferred stocks issued by Strategy Corporation (NASDAQ: MSTR)

Strategy Preferred Equities are fixed-income instruments issued by Strategy Corporation — the company that holds more Bitcoin than any other publicly traded firm in the world. Each preferred pays a stated dividend and trades on NASDAQ like a stock.

Unlike traditional bonds, these are perpetual preferreds — no maturity date. Strategy can optionally redeem them at par ($100) at any time. When they trade below $100, buyers capture both the dividend yield and the discount-recovery upside when or if the price returns to par.

Think of them as a way to earn a high yield while Bitcoin appreciates underneath — without directly owning BTC or dealing with exchanges.

"Buying STRK at a discount is like buying a 10% coupon bond at 15-20% yield-to-maturity. When BTC doubles and the discount closes to par, you capture both the yield and the price appreciation."— Michael Saylor, Executive Chairman, Strategy Corporation

STRC —

Stretch — Variable Monthly at Par
~11.5%
Eff. Yield at Par

On STRC: Michael Saylor has described STRC as the preferred that keeps paying — a variable-rate instrument with a monthly adjustable coupon tied to short-term financing costs. When other prefs have fixed coupons that look stale in a changing rate environment, STRC's coupon resets, making it the most resilient structure for income seekers who want exposure to Bitcoin's eventual appreciation. As a monthly-pay instrument near or above par, STRC functions as a near-cash equivalent for investors who want constant income while preserving the option to deploy capital into Bitcoin at opportune moments.

STRK —

Strike — 10% Fixed Quarterly
10.00%
Eff. Yield at Par

On STRK: Saylor has called STRK the cleanest expression of the preferred strategy — a fixed 10% coupon paid quarterly at $100 par. When STRK traded at a discount, he pointed to the 10% coupon as an embedded discount capture opportunity, noting that if BTC doubles and the discount closes, STRK holders capture both the yield and the price appreciation. STRK is the flagship preferred: known record dates, predictable cash flow, and the Saylor-endorsed vehicle for deploying capital into the next Bitcoin cycle.

STRD —

Strike Discount — 10% Fixed Quarterly
14–15%
Eff. Yield at Discount

On STRD: Saylor has highlighted STRD as the maximum yield opportunity within the preferred family — the same 10% coupon as STRK but trading at a significant discount to par. He has argued that buying STRD at $80-$85 is effectively buying a 10% coupon bond at 15-20% yield-to-maturity, with Bitcoin's eventual appreciation as the catalyst to close the discount to $100. STRD represents the highest income return in the family for investors willing to accept the discount risk as the price of that yield premium.

STRF —

On STRF: Saylor has described STRF as the flexible friend — a variable-rate preferred designed to reprice quarterly based on a formula tied to market conditions. When BTC is in a correction and fixed rates look rich, STRF offers downside protection through its variable structure. As rates eventually normalize and the preferred structure reprices, STRF's flexibility makes it the hold-through-any-environment instrument, particularly suited for investors who want the preferred income but are uncertain about the rate cycle.

STRE —

Stream (EU) — 10% Quarterly / EUR-Hedged
12%+
Eff. Yield (USD)

On STRE: Saylor has called STRE the European opportunity — a euro-denominated preferred issued out of Strategy's EU subsidiary, paying 10% quarterly in EUR. For non-US investors holding EUR, STRE avoids currency risk entirely while delivering the same 10% coupon as STRK. Saylor has framed it as the preferred most suited for international family offices and European institutional investors who want BTC exposure through a familiar fixed-income instrument, noting that when EUR/USD moves favorably, STRE holders capture both the coupon and currency appreciation.
